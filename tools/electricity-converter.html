Voltage When Moving Abroad

The US and Canada use 110-120V at 60Hz. Most of Europe, including Portugal, uses 220-240V at 50Hz. Many modern electronics (phones, laptops) are dual-voltage (100-240V) and only need a plug adapter. High-wattage appliances like hair dryers, electric kettles, and kitchen appliances are usually single-voltage and require a proper voltage converter.

  • Check the label: "INPUT: 100-240V" = works everywhere with just an adapter
  • Single voltage appliances: Need a step-up/step-down converter (heavy, expensive)
  • Best solution: Buy high-wattage appliances locally at destination
