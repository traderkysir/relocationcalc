International Health Insurance for Expats

International health insurance typically costs $100–600/month for a single adult depending on age and coverage level. Many countries require proof of health coverage as part of their long-term visa application process, including Portugal's D7 and Spain's non-lucrative visa.

  • Basic hospital coverage: Good for emergencies, lower cost
  • Comprehensive plans: Include outpatient, dental, vision, and mental health
  • US-included plans: Most expensive due to US healthcare costs — avoid if you won't need US coverage
